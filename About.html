Purpose

For those who seek for light in the dark.

Nightscape is a company based around stopping anti consumer practises by astronomy equipment manufacturers. The costs of telescopes have risen 17% in the last 5 years according to the Hayden Planetarium. But material costs have dropped 30% according Saudi ArmCo Petro Chemicals. This high cost of entry can be off putting for most people, this is harming the rapidly growing space sector.

According to NASA jobs in the space sector need to grow 10% to maintain current progress speeds. This is where we come in, Nightscape provides cheap remote observatories to customers. The customers use their laptops or smart phones to control telescopes at a fraction of the cost.





































How do we help?

Collecting drops of light in bucket.

Nightscape granst users access to a 5 inch telescope. The telescope uses a CCD camera to transmit images to a server which then allocates the images to repective users. The sensor of the camera is as big as the image circle of the telescope. This means that the users is seeing everything they would see with an eyepiece.

The photos collected can be sent to scientific institutes on request of the user. Users can select targets from solar system to deep sky. The lost cost of enerty also means that users can experimental and gain new skills which will help them get a job in the space sector.
